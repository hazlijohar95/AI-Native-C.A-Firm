First Principles

What is a Sdn Bhd, really?

A Sendirian Berhad (Sdn Bhd) is a private limited company — the most popular business structure in Malaysia. Here's why entrepreneurs choose it:

1

Separate Legal Entity

Your company is legally separate from you. It can own property, sign contracts, hire employees, and take on debt in its own name.

2

Limited Liability

Your personal assets (house, car, savings) are protected. If the company fails, shareholders are only liable up to their capital contribution.

3

Tax Advantages

Corporate tax rates (17-24%) can be lower than personal tax. Access to tax incentives, grants, and deductions only available to Sdn Bhd companies.

4

Business Credibility

Banks, investors, and clients take you seriously. Easier to get loans (up to 4x more than Enterprise), raise capital, and win contracts.

Basic Requirements

  • At least 1 director (Malaysian resident)
  • At least 1 shareholder (individual or company)

FAQ

Common questions

Everything you need to know about incorporating in Malaysia

How long does incorporation take?
Most companies are registered within 3-7 business days once all documents are submitted correctly. Complex structures or name issues may take longer.
Can a foreigner be the sole director?
Yes, if they are a Malaysian resident with a valid work permit. Otherwise, you'll need at least one local resident director — we can provide this service in our Complete package.
What's the minimum paid-up capital required?
Just RM 1. However, some industries and foreign ownership structures may require higher capital. We'll advise you on your specific situation.
Do I need a physical office address?
Yes, every Sdn Bhd needs a registered address in Malaysia. We can provide this service in our Business and Complete packages.
What happens after incorporation?
After receiving your Certificate of Incorporation, you'll need to open a corporate bank account, register for taxes (if applicable), and maintain annual compliance. We guide you through all of this.
What's the difference between an Enterprise and Sdn Bhd?
An Enterprise (sole proprietorship) has unlimited liability — your personal assets are at risk. A Sdn Bhd has limited liability, separate legal entity status, and better access to funding and tax incentives.
