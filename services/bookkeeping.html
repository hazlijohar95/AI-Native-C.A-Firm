First Principles

What is bookkeeping, really?

At its core, bookkeeping is the systematic recording of every financial transaction your business makes. It's the foundation of all financial decisions.

1

Capture Every Transaction

Every sale, purchase, payment, and receipt must be recorded. Missing even one transaction creates blind spots in your financial picture.

2

Categorize Correctly

Each transaction needs to be classified (e.g., "Office Supplies" vs "Marketing"). Correct categorization enables accurate reporting and tax deductions.

3

Reconcile Regularly

Match your records against bank statements to ensure accuracy. Reconciliation catches errors, prevents fraud, and keeps your books audit-ready.

4

Generate Reports

Transform raw data into financial statements (P&L, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow). These reports show you where you stand and where you're heading.
